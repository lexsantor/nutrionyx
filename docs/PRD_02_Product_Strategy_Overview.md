# 02. Product Strategy & Overview

## 2.1 Problem Statement

Nutrition professionals often rely on disconnected tools such as
spreadsheets, messaging apps, PDFs and paper questionnaires. This
fragmentation reduces efficiency, increases errors and makes long-term
patient follow-up difficult.

Patients also struggle to maintain adherence because relevant
information is scattered across multiple channels and progress is not
visualized in a motivating way.

## 2.2 Opportunity

Create a single platform that centralizes every interaction between
nutritionist and patient, transforming clinical follow-up into a
continuous digital experience.

The platform should become the daily operating system for nutrition
professionals rather than a simple record-keeping application.

## 2.3 Value Proposition

### For Nutritionists

-   Centralized patient management.
-   Personalized treatment plans.
-   Longitudinal progress tracking.
-   Reduced administrative work.
-   Actionable dashboards.

### For Patients

-   Private access to their treatment.
-   Clear understanding of progress.
-   Easy tracking of measurements and medication.
-   Greater engagement through visual feedback.

## 2.4 Product Positioning

The product is positioned as a premium nutrition management platform.

It competes through usability, simplicity and long-term scalability
rather than feature count.

## 2.5 Business Objectives

-   Build a scalable multi-tenant SaaS.
-   Support multiple nutrition practices.
-   Minimize operational costs.
-   Enable future subscription plans.
-   Reduce maintenance through a unified architecture.

## 2.6 User Objectives

Nutritionists should be able to:

-   onboard patients quickly
-   monitor progress efficiently
-   adjust treatment with minimal effort

Patients should be able to:

-   understand their treatment
-   record progress effortlessly
-   remain engaged over time

## 2.7 Product Success Metrics

### Business KPIs

-   Monthly Active Nutritionists
-   Monthly Active Patients
-   Patient retention
-   Clinic retention
-   Feature adoption

### Product KPIs

-   Assessment completion rate
-   Weekly active patients
-   Medication adherence
-   Weight log frequency
-   Body measurement frequency
-   Exercise adherence

## 2.8 North Star Metric

Percentage of active patients who consistently complete their treatment
tracking over a rolling 30-day period.

## 2.9 Product Differentiators

-   Multi-tenant by design.
-   Premium UX.
-   Unified clinical workflow.
-   Secure patient isolation.
-   Modular architecture.
-   Future-ready for AI and wearable integrations.

## 2.10 Success Criteria

The first release will be considered successful when:

-   A nutritionist can manage all patients from a single workspace.
-   Patients can independently complete onboarding and follow-up.
-   All information remains isolated per organization.
-   Core workflows require minimal training.
-   The platform is ready for iterative expansion without architectural
    redesign.
